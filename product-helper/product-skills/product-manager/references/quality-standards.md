# Quality Standards & Benchmarks

Minimum quality standards, role-specific deliverables, and benchmark criteria applied across
all three product skills at kick-offs, check-ins, and handovers.

---

## How to Use This Document

These standards define:
- **Minimum quality bar (MQB)**: the floor below which work should not proceed
- **Benchmark**: the expected standard for solid, professional work at that stage
- **Excellence marker**: what distinguishes exceptional work

All three skills reference this document. Standards apply regardless of team size, methodology,
or tooling. They scale — a solo designer's handover and a 10-person team's handover both need
the same information present, even if the format differs.

---

## Part 1 — Universal Quality Standards (All Roles)

These apply at every stage, for every role.

### 1.1 — Problem Clarity

| Standard | MQB | Benchmark | Excellence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Goal stated | ✅ An outcome is described | ✅ Outcome is measurable and time-bounded | ✅ Outcome is tied to a strategic metric with a baseline |
| Problem statement | ✅ A problem is named | ✅ User + barrier + impact, solution-free | ✅ Validated with direct user evidence |
| Why now | ✅ Some rationale given | ✅ Specific urgency signal or strategic window | ✅ Cost of delay quantified or clearly reasoned |
| User identified | ✅ A user type is named | ✅ Specific segment with known context | ✅ Primary vs secondary users distinguished |

### 1.2 — Signal Quality

| Standard | MQB | Benchmark | Excellence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Evidence present | ✅ Some basis for the work | ✅ Mix of Tier 1–3 signals | ✅ Tier 1–2 primary, Tier 3–4 supporting |
| Assumptions explicit | ✅ Key assumptions identified | ✅ Assumptions categorised and ranked | ✅ Highest-risk assumptions have validation plans |
| Research recency | ✅ Evidence exists | ✅ Evidence is <12 months old | ✅ Research ongoing or recent round completed |

### 1.3 — Traceability

| Standard | MQB | Benchmark | Excellence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insight chain | ✅ Direction has some rationale | ✅ Signal → Insight → Opportunity → Direction traceable | ✅ Chain documented explicitly with sources |
| Decisions recorded | ✅ Key decisions are known | ✅ Decisions recorded with rationale | ✅ Alternatives considered and documented |
| Out-of-scope defined | ✅ Some boundaries exist | ✅ Explicit out-of-scope list with reasoning | ✅ Out-of-scope linked to phasing plan |

## Part 2 — Product Strategy Standards

### 2.1 — Kick-off Deliverables (PS)

Required before strategy work begins:

| Deliverable | MQB | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic context | One paragraph framing the opportunity | Full context: market, timing, competitive landscape |
| Goal or north star | An outcome described | Measurable, tied to business objective |
| Scope of strategy work | What questions are being answered | What is and isn't being decided |
| Stakeholder map | Who is involved | Who decides, who advises, who is informed |
| Known constraints | At least one constraint named | Budget, timeline, platform, team scope all addressed |
| Success definition | How we'll know the strategy worked | Specific, measurable success criteria with timeframe |

### 2.2 — In-Progress Standards (PS)

At any check-in during strategy work:

| Standard | MQB | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Opportunity framing | An opportunity can be described | Opportunity is grounded in evidence, not intuition |
| Assumption register | Key assumptions are listed | Assumptions ranked by importance and evidence |
| Signal inventory | Evidence is referenced | Tier 1–3 signals dominate; Tier 5–6 flagged |
| Alternative directions | At least one alternative considered | 3+ directions explored with trade-offs noted |
| Stakeholder alignment | Key stakeholders aware of work | Agreement on problem statement at minimum |

### 2.3 — Handover Deliverables (PS → PM)

What Product Strategy must produce before handing to Product Manager:

| Deliverable | MQB | Benchmark | Excellence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | Outcome described | Measurable + time-bounded | Tied to strategic metric with baseline |
| **Problem statement** | User + problem named | User + barrier + impact, solution-free | Validated with evidence, iterated once |
| **Why now** | Rationale present | Specific urgency signal or strategic window | Cost of delay reasoned with supporting data |
| **Strategic context** | Background present | Market, competitive, timing context | Quantified opportunity size where possible |
| **Success criteria** | What success looks like | Specific metrics with timeframes | Leading and lagging indicators defined |
| **Assumptions register** | Key assumptions listed | Ranked on 2D map | Top-right quadrant has validation recommendations |
| **Scope boundaries** | What is in scope | What is in AND out of scope with reasoning | Phase 1 bounded, future phases outlined |
| **Open questions** | Key unknowns noted | Questions organised by priority | Questions assigned to PM discovery plan |
| **Stakeholder context** | Key stakeholders named | Stakeholder positions and concerns noted | Alignment gaps flagged explicitly |

**Handover MQB**: Goal + Problem statement + Why now + Scope boundaries must all be present.
If any are missing, the handover should not proceed.

## Part 3 — Product Manager Standards

### 3.1 — Kick-off Deliverables (PM)

Required before discovery or definition work begins:

| Deliverable | MQB | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Problem statement | Named and accepted | Reviewed and refined with strategy input |
| Discovery plan | What methods will be used | Methods + timelines + participant profiles |
| Research questions | Key unknowns named | Questions prioritised against assumptions |
| Stakeholder map | Who is involved | Decision rights, sign-off process, review points |
| Constraints inventory | Known constraints listed | Constraints assessed for impact on scope |
| Definition of done | When is discovery complete | Specific outputs required to conclude discovery |

### 3.2 — In-Progress Standards (PM)

At any check-in during discovery or definition:

| Standard | MQB | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery progress | Research is underway | Research complete or on track with specific findings |
| Insight formation | Raw data being collected | Data synthesised into insights (not just quotes) |
| Assumption testing | Assumptions identified | Highest-risk assumptions being tested |
| Scope stability | Scope roughly understood | Scope documented, out-of-scope explicit |
| Requirements forming | User needs being identified | Needs organised into requirements framework |

### 3.3 — Handover Deliverables (PM → PD)

What Product Manager must produce before handing to Product Designer:

| Deliverable | MQB | Benchmark | Excellence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Problem statement** | Clear and validated | Iterated from research findings | Includes direct user quotes as evidence |
| **User needs** | Primary needs listed | Needs framed as JTBD, ranked by importance | Needs validated with multiple user sources |
| **User profiles** | User type described | 1–2 specific profiles with context and goals | Profiles grounded in research, not assumed |
| **Success metrics** | How we'll measure success | Specific metrics + targets | Primary metric + supporting metrics + guardrails |
| **Requirements** | User needs + business requirements | Full requirements: user, business, technical, a11y | Requirements ranked (must/should/could) with rationale |
| **Scope for this phase** | What's being designed | V1 scope clearly bounded | Out-of-scope listed + future phase outline |
| **Known constraints** | Key constraints named | Technical, platform, accessibility constraints | Constraints assessed + design implications noted |
| **Assumptions** | Key design assumptions listed | Ranked by importance and evidence | Highest-risk assumptions flagged for testing |
| **Open questions** | Key unknowns for design | Questions organised by priority + type | Questions assigned to design research plan |
| **Research findings** | Insights available | Full synthesis: insights + implications | Research artefacts accessible + implications clear |

**Handover MQB**: Problem statement + User needs + Success metrics + V1 scope + Known constraints
must all be present. If any are missing, the handover should not proceed.

### 3.4 — Handover Deliverables (PM — General / Spec handover)

When PM hands a spec or requirements document to any role:

| Deliverable | MQB | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Scope clearly bounded | What is included | What is included + explicitly excluded |
| Requirements traceable | Requirements listed | Each requirement linked to a user need or business goal |
| Acceptance criteria | How we'll know it's done | Specific, testable criteria for each requirement |
| Edge cases noted | Known edge cases listed | Edge cases prioritised with design/eng implications |

## Part 4 — Product Designer Standards

### 4.1 — Kick-off Deliverables (PD)

Required before design work begins:

| Deliverable | MQB | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Design brief | Problem and user understood | Full brief: problem, user, needs, constraints, success |
| Fidelity plan | Expected output described | Fidelity per phase with rationale and timeline |
| Design constraints | Key constraints known | All constraints: technical, platform, a11y, system |
| Testing approach | How design will be validated | Methods + timing + participant plan |
| Design system context | Whether a system exists | Components to use, gaps to address, new patterns to create |
| Review + approval process | Who will review | Reviewers identified, review points scheduled |

### 4.2 — In-Progress Standards (PD)

At any check-in during design:

| Standard | MQB (Lo-fi) | Benchmark (Mid-fi) | Benchmark (Hi-fi) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Problem grounding | Design responds to stated problem | Design decisions traceable to user needs | Design validated against user feedback |
| Flow completeness | Happy path exists | Happy + error + empty paths exist | All states + edge cases + branch paths |
| Fidelity appropriateness | Fidelity matches stated stage | Fidelity appropriate for next planned action | Fidelity appropriate + reviewers aligned |
| Annotations | Key decisions noted | Interactions and behaviours annotated | Full annotation including states, tokens, behaviour |
| Design system | Existing components considered | Existing components used where appropriate | System alignment verified; new patterns justified |

### 4.3 — Handover Deliverables (PD → Engineering)

What Product Designer must produce before handing to Engineering:

| Deliverable | MQB | Benchmark | Excellence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **All screen states** | Default state present | Default + error + empty + loading | All: default, hover, active, focus, disabled, error, empty, loading, skeleton |
| **User flows** | Happy path complete | Happy + error + alternative paths | All flows incl. edge cases, access states, first-time vs returning |
| **Annotations** | Key behaviours noted | All interactions annotated | Full: behaviour, tokens, measurements, conditional logic |
| **Component spec** | Components identified | Components named + mapped to design system | Components documented with tokens, variants, states |
| **Responsive behaviour** | Mobile + desktop addressed | Breakpoints specified | All breakpoints + overflow/truncation/edge cases |
| **Accessibility** | Colour contrast checked | WCAG AA compliance for key flows | Full WCAG AA + keyboard nav + screen reader notes |
| **Assets** | Key assets present | All assets exported at correct sizes | Assets named, organised, format specified |
| **Open questions** | Known unknowns listed | Questions organised + engineering implications | Questions triaged with recommended resolutions |
| **Design decisions log** | Key decisions accessible | Rationale for non-obvious decisions noted | Full decision log with alternatives considered |

**Handover MQB**: All screen states + user flows (happy + error + empty) + annotations +
component spec + accessibility basics must be complete. If any are missing, the handover
should not proceed.

### 4.4 — Handover Deliverables (PD → Stakeholder Review)

Before presenting for stakeholder sign-off:

| Deliverable | MQB | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| Problem statement visible | Referenced in presentation | Explicitly shown as design context |
| User needs addressed | Each need has a design response | Needs-to-design mapping demonstrated |
| Key decisions explained | Design decisions noted | Rationale and alternatives communicated |
| What is not being solved | Known gaps mentioned | Out-of-scope explicit with reasoning |
| Next steps clear | What happens after review | Specific actions, owners, and timelines |

## Part 5 — Cross-Role Handover Standards

### 5.1 — What Every Handover Must Include

Regardless of roles involved:

1. **Context**: What problem is being solved and why
2. **Current state**: What has been done and what decisions have been made
3. **Deliverables**: Everything the receiving role needs, complete and accessible
4. **Open questions**: What is unresolved, organised by priority
5. **Assumptions**: What is being assumed that has not been validated
6. **Known risks**: What could go wrong and why
7. **Definition of next stage**: What the receiving role should produce and by when

### 5.2 — Handover Quality Gates

A handover should be challenged if:
- ❌ The problem statement is missing or solution-shaped
- ❌ The receiving role's primary deliverables are undefined
- ❌ No success criteria have been established
- ❌ Open questions have not been documented
- ❌ The receiving role has not confirmed they have what they need
- ❌ Key assumptions are unstated or untested

### 5.3 — Kick-off Quality Gates

A kick-off should be challenged if:
- ❌ The team does not share a common understanding of the problem
- ❌ The goal is not stated in outcome terms
- ❌ Constraints (time, scope, technical, resource) have not been discussed
- ❌ Roles and responsibilities are unclear
- ❌ There is no agreed definition of done for this phase
- ❌ Key assumptions have not been surfaced

## Part 6 — Minimum Quality Bar Summary

Quick reference: what must exist before work can proceed to the next stage.

| Transition | Must haves |
|---|---|
| **Before discovery starts** | Problem hypothesis, user identified, discovery questions defined |
| **Before design starts** | Problem statement (validated), user needs, success metrics, V1 scope, constraints |
| **Before hi-fi design starts** | Lo/mid-fi validated with users or stakeholders, flows complete, system aligned |
| **Before engineering handoff** | All states, all flows, full annotation, component spec, a11y baseline |
| **Before launch** | Acceptance criteria met, edge cases covered, monitoring plan in place |
| **Before strategy is locked** | Goal, problem statement, why now, assumptions registered |
