# Product Strategy — Task List

A stage-by-stage guide for Product Strategy work. Work through each phase in order.
Tasks marked **[MQB]** are Minimum Quality Bar — they must be complete before progressing.

---

## Phase 1 — Kick-off

Tasks to complete before strategy work begins in earnest.

### Align on the opportunity
- [ ] Write a one-paragraph description of the opportunity or problem being explored
- [ ] Identify what triggered this work now — what has changed or become possible
- [ ] Name the users or customer segments this strategy is ultimately for
- [ ] Connect this work to a specific business objective
- [ ] State what would happen if this strategy is wrong or skipped

### Establish the goal
- [ ] Draft a goal framed as an outcome, not a feature or output
- [ ] Define how success will be measured in 6 and 12 months
- [ ] Distinguish the minimum acceptable outcome from an ideal one

### Surface constraints and assumptions
- [ ] List known constraints: time, budget, team capacity, technical, regulatory
- [ ] Write down what you believe to be true that has not yet been validated
- [ ] State what would have to be true for this strategy to succeed
- [ ] Name what could make this the wrong direction entirely

### Confirm alignment and process
- [ ] Identify who needs to be aligned or consulted for this strategy to be adopted
- [ ] Name who has final decision-making authority over the strategic direction
- [ ] Define what done looks like for this phase of strategy work

**Kick-off gate**: Do not proceed until the opportunity, goal, and stakeholder alignment are established.

---

## Phase 2 — Research & Signal Gathering

Tasks for building an evidence base before forming a direction.

### Inventory existing evidence
- [ ] List all existing research, data, or signals relevant to this problem space
- [ ] Assess the tier of each signal (1 = empirical data → 6 = untested assumption)
- [ ] Flag any signals older than 12 months for verification
- [ ] Note where the evidence is strong and where it is thin

### Identify what is unknown
- [ ] List the top 5 unknowns that could invalidate the strategy
- [ ] Rank unknowns by impact if wrong
- [ ] Determine which unknowns require new research vs can be inferred
- [ ] Assign an owner and method to each critical unknown

### Build the insight chain
- [ ] For each key signal, write the insight it produces (what does it mean?)
- [ ] For each insight, identify the opportunity it opens
- [ ] For each opportunity, write a candidate direction
- [ ] Check that no direction has been stated without a traceable signal

### Competitive and market context
- [ ] Research how competitors or analogous products address this problem space
- [ ] Note market or timing conditions relevant to 'why now'
- [ ] Identify any regulatory, platform, or external changes that affect the strategy

---

## Phase 3 — Strategy Formation

Tasks for developing and stress-testing the strategic direction.

### Write the core strategy elements

- [ ] **[MQB]** Write the goal as a measurable outcome with a timeframe
- [ ] **[MQB]** Write the problem statement: *[User] trying to [goal] but [barrier], which means [impact]*
- [ ] **[MQB]** Write the 'why now' statement with a specific urgency signal or strategic window
- [ ] Define success criteria with specific metrics and a timeframe
- [ ] Define what is in scope and explicitly what is out of scope

### Map and rank assumptions
- [ ] List every assumption the strategy depends on
- [ ] Score each assumption: importance (1–5) and evidence strength (1–5)
- [ ] Plot assumptions on the 2D map (importance vs evidence)
- [ ] For each top-right quadrant assumption (important + weak evidence): write a validation plan
- [ ] Flag any assumption that, if wrong, would invalidate the strategy entirely

### Stress-test the direction
- [ ] Identify at least two alternative directions that were considered
- [ ] Document why each alternative was not chosen
- [ ] Write the strongest possible argument against the chosen direction
- [ ] Confirm the chosen direction holds up against that argument

### Validate with stakeholders
- [ ] Share the problem statement with at least one key stakeholder for feedback
- [ ] Share the goal and success criteria with the business objective owner
- [ ] Incorporate any material feedback into the strategy document
- [ ] Note any unresolved stakeholder disagreements as open questions

---

## Phase 4 — Documentation

Tasks for producing the handover-ready strategy artefact.

- [ ] Write the strategic context section (market, timing, competitive landscape)
- [ ] Document the goal (outcome, measure, timeframe)
- [ ] Document the problem statement (validated version)
- [ ] Document the 'why now' rationale
- [ ] Document the success criteria (metrics + timeframes)
- [ ] Document the scope: what is in and explicitly what is out
- [ ] Include the assumptions register with the 2D map
- [ ] List the top open questions for PM to answer through discovery
- [ ] Include stakeholder context: who is aligned, who has concerns, who decides
- [ ] Write a brief summary of alternatives considered and why they were not chosen

---

## Phase 5 — Handover to Product Manager

Tasks for preparing and executing a quality handover.

### Pre-handover checklist
- [ ] **[MQB]** Goal is written as a measurable outcome ✓
- [ ] **[MQB]** Problem statement follows user + barrier + impact format, no solution present ✓
- [ ] **[MQB]** Why now is specific — names a signal, window, or cost of delay ✓
- [ ] **[MQB]** Scope boundaries are defined — both in-scope and out-of-scope ✓
- [ ] Success criteria are defined with metrics and a timeframe ✓
- [ ] Assumptions register is complete and ranked ✓
- [ ] Top 3 open questions for PM are listed ✓
- [ ] Stakeholder context is documented ✓
- [ ] PM has access to all research and context used in strategy ✓

### Handover conversation
- [ ] Walk PM through the problem statement — don't just send the document
- [ ] Highlight the top-right quadrant assumptions that PM should validate or challenge
- [ ] Confirm PM agrees with and accepts the problem statement
- [ ] Confirm PM understands the strategic constraints and scope boundaries
- [ ] Agree on what PM will produce and by when

### Post-handover
- [ ] Record the handover date and what was transferred
- [ ] Remain available for PM questions during discovery
- [ ] Schedule a check-in with PM at the midpoint of discovery
- [ ] Plan to review the problem statement after discovery to confirm it still holds

---

## Ongoing — Quality Self-Checks

Use these questions at any point during strategy work to check quality:

- Can I articulate the opportunity in one clear sentence?
- Is the goal an outcome or is it an output in disguise?
- Does the problem statement name a user, a barrier, and an impact — with no solution?
- Is there a 'why now' stronger than "it was requested" or "it's been on the backlog"?
- Are the signals driving this work Tier 1–3, or primarily opinion and assumption?
- Can I trace the path from signal → insight → opportunity → direction → decision?
- What are the top three things that could make this strategy wrong?
- Have I considered and documented at least two alternative directions?
- Do the key stakeholders agree on the problem statement?
- Is there anything in this strategy I am not comfortable with or uncertain about?
