# Checkpoint Question Banks

Structured question sets for kick-offs, check-ins, and handovers across all three product roles.

These questions are designed to:
- Surface misalignment and gaps early
- Ensure the right thinking has happened before the right work begins
- Create a shared record of decisions, assumptions, and open questions
- Hold work to industry-standard quality benchmarks

---

## How These Questions Work

Questions are grouped by **role** and **checkpoint type**. Each question has a purpose tag:
- `[alignment]` — checks shared understanding
- `[quality]` — checks standard of work produced
- `[risk]` — surfaces assumptions, blockers, and gaps
- `[readiness]` — checks whether the work or team is ready to proceed
- `[scope]` — establishes or validates boundaries

Not every question applies to every situation. The skill selects and adapts based on context.

## Product Strategy Questions

### Kick-off Questions (PS)

Use when starting a strategy piece, opportunity exploration, or strategic direction-setting.

**Understanding the strategic context:**
1. What specific opportunity, problem, or question is this strategy work addressing? `[alignment]`
2. What triggered this work now — what has changed or become possible? `[risk]`
3. Who are the users or customers this strategy is ultimately for? `[alignment]`
4. What business objective does this connect to? `[alignment]`
5. What would it mean if we got this strategy wrong? `[risk]`

**Establishing the goal:**
6. What outcome are we trying to achieve — not what we will build, but what will change? `[quality]`
7. How will we measure whether the strategy succeeded in 6 or 12 months? `[quality]`
8. What is the minimum acceptable outcome, versus what would be a great outcome? `[scope]`

**Surfacing constraints and assumptions:**
9. What are the known constraints — time, budget, team capacity, technical, regulatory? `[scope]`
10. What do we believe to be true about the market, users, or situation that we haven't validated? `[risk]`
11. What would have to be true for this strategy to succeed? `[risk]`
12. What could make this the wrong direction entirely? `[risk]`

**Alignment and process:**
13. Who needs to be aligned or consulted for this strategy to be adopted? `[alignment]`
14. Who has decision-making authority over this strategic direction? `[alignment]`
15. What does done look like for this phase of strategy work? `[readiness]`

---

### Check-in Questions (PS)

Use when reviewing strategy work in progress or when asked if work is on track.

**Work quality:**
1. Can you articulate the opportunity in one clear sentence? `[quality]`
2. Is the goal framed as an outcome — not an output or a feature? `[quality]`
3. Does the problem statement name a user, describe a barrier, and state an impact? `[quality]`
4. Is there a clear 'why now' that goes beyond "it was requested"? `[quality]`

**Signal and evidence:**
5. What evidence supports the claim that this problem is worth solving? `[quality]`
6. How fresh is the evidence — is it based on recent research or data? `[risk]`
7. Are you acting on strong signals (Tier 1–3) or primarily on opinion and assumption (Tier 4–6)? `[risk]`
8. What assumptions are you making that you haven't yet validated? `[risk]`

**Direction and reasoning:**
9. How did you get from the signal to the proposed direction — can you trace the chain? `[quality]`
10. What alternative directions were considered and why were they not chosen? `[quality]`
11. What is explicitly out of scope for this strategy, and why? `[scope]`

**Progress and readiness:**
12. What are the open questions you still need to answer before the strategy is ready to hand over? `[readiness]`
13. Who has reviewed this work and what feedback has been incorporated? `[quality]`
14. Is there anything in the work you are uncertain about or not comfortable with? `[risk]`

---

### Handover Questions (PS → PM)

Use when strategy work is being handed to a Product Manager to begin discovery and definition.

**Completeness check:**
1. Is the goal written as a measurable outcome? `[quality]`
2. Does the problem statement follow the format: user + barrier + impact, with no solution? `[quality]`
3. Is there a credible 'why now' — with a specific urgency signal or strategic window? `[quality]`
4. Are success criteria defined with specific metrics and a timeframe? `[quality]`

**What PM needs to receive:**
5. What is explicitly in scope for the PM to discover and define? `[scope]`
6. What is explicitly out of scope, and has that been communicated to PM? `[scope]`
7. What are the top 3 open questions the PM needs to answer through discovery? `[readiness]`
8. What assumptions has strategy made that PM should validate or challenge? `[risk]`
9. What stakeholder context does PM need to navigate this work? `[alignment]`

**Alignment:**
10. Has PM reviewed the problem statement and confirmed they understand it? `[alignment]`
11. Are there any strategic constraints PM needs to design discovery around? `[scope]`
12. Does PM have access to all the research and context that strategy used? `[readiness]`

## Product Manager Questions

### Kick-off Questions (PM)

Use when starting discovery, definition, or requirements work.

**Understanding the problem:**
1. What is the problem statement we are starting with — is it specific, user-centred, and free of solutions? `[alignment]`
2. Do you agree with this problem statement, or does it need to be refined before work starts? `[alignment]`
3. Who is the primary user we are solving for — and what is their context and goal? `[alignment]`
4. What do we currently know about this problem from research or data? `[quality]`
5. What are the biggest unknowns — what do we most need to find out? `[risk]`

**Planning discovery:**
6. What research methods will be used, and why are they appropriate for this problem? `[quality]`
7. Who will be involved in research — what participant profiles and how many? `[quality]`
8. What questions are we taking into research — and are they open-ended or leading? `[quality]`
9. What assumptions are we testing, and how will we know if they are valid or invalid? `[risk]`
10. What is the timeline for discovery, and what will trigger the end of discovery? `[readiness]`

**Constraints and scope:**
11. What technical, business, or platform constraints must the solution work within? `[scope]`
12. What is the scope of the V1 — and is that scope coming from strategy, or does PM need to define it? `[scope]`
13. What will not be addressed in this phase, and is that agreed? `[scope]`

**Stakeholders and process:**
14. Who needs to be informed or consulted during discovery? `[alignment]`
15. Who has sign-off authority for the requirements and problem definition? `[alignment]`
16. What does done look like for this PM phase — what must be produced before design begins? `[readiness]`

---

### Check-in Questions (PM)

Use during discovery or definition, or when asked if work is on track.

**Discovery quality:**
1. What have you learned from users so far — not what they said, but what it means? `[quality]`
2. Have you synthesised the research into insights, or are you still working with raw data? `[quality]`
3. Are the insights challenging your assumptions, confirming them, or both? `[risk]`
4. What surprised you in the research? `[quality]`
5. Is the problem statement still accurate, or has research changed your understanding? `[quality]`

**Requirements and scope:**
6. Are user needs framed as jobs-to-be-done — not as features or UI elements? `[quality]`
7. Are business requirements distinct from user needs, and are they ranked? `[quality]`
8. Is the V1 scope bounded with a clear rationale for what's in and out? `[scope]`
9. Are there any requirements that are actually solutions in disguise? `[risk]`

**Readiness for design:**
10. Does design have everything they need to start work — problem, needs, constraints, success criteria? `[readiness]`
11. Are there open questions that should be answered before design starts? `[risk]`
12. Are the highest-risk assumptions flagged for testing during or after design? `[risk]`

---

### Handover Questions (PM → PD)

Use when PM is handing work to a Product Designer.

**Completeness check:**
1. Is the problem statement clear, validated, and free of solutions? `[quality]`
2. Are user needs framed as what users are trying to accomplish — not as features? `[quality]`
3. Is the success metric specific — what will change, by how much, and by when? `[quality]`
4. Is the V1 scope defined with explicit out-of-scope items? `[scope]`
5. Are technical and platform constraints documented with design implications? `[scope]`

**What PD needs to receive:**
6. Does the designer understand who they are designing for and what those users are trying to do? `[alignment]`
7. Does the designer know what success looks like and how it will be measured? `[alignment]`
8. Are there any stakeholder constraints or preferences PD needs to know? `[alignment]`
9. What are the top assumptions PD should test during design or prototyping? `[risk]`
10. What open questions remain for design to address or explore? `[readiness]`

**Research handover:**
11. Does design have access to the research findings and synthesis? `[readiness]`
12. Have you walked design through the key insights — not just sent a document? `[alignment]`
13. Are there specific user quotes or observations that should directly inform design? `[quality]`

## Product Designer Questions

### Kick-off Questions (PD)

Use when beginning design work on a problem.

**Understanding what to design:**
1. What problem is this design work solving — can you describe it in one sentence without mentioning a feature? `[alignment]`
2. Who is the primary user, and what are they trying to accomplish? `[alignment]`
3. What user needs must this design address — and which are must-have versus nice-to-have? `[quality]`
4. What does success look like — how will we know this design is working after launch? `[quality]`

**Scope and fidelity:**
5. What is in scope for this design phase — and what is explicitly not being designed yet? `[scope]`
6. What fidelity is expected and by what point — and what is the rationale? `[scope]`
7. What will the design output be used for — exploration, user testing, stakeholder review, or engineering handoff? `[readiness]`

**Constraints:**
8. What technical or platform constraints must the design work within? `[scope]`
9. Are there existing components, patterns, or system guidelines this design should use or extend? `[quality]`
10. What accessibility requirements apply to this work? `[quality]`

**Process:**
11. How will the design be validated — is user testing planned, and at what fidelity? `[quality]`
12. Who will review the design and at what points — and what are they reviewing for? `[alignment]`
13. What does done look like for this design phase before handover? `[readiness]`

---

### Check-in Questions (PD)

Use during design work, or when asked if work is on track.

**Design grounding:**
1. Is each significant design decision traceable to a user need or evidence? `[quality]`
2. Are you designing for the problem as stated, or has the problem shifted — and if so, is PM aware? `[alignment]`
3. Have you explored more than one approach before committing to this direction? `[quality]`

**Completeness:**
4. Beyond the happy path, have you considered what happens when things go wrong — error states, empty states, edge cases? `[quality]`
5. Are all decision branches in the flow resolved? `[quality]`
6. Is the fidelity appropriate for what this work is being used for right now? `[quality]`

**System and consistency:**
7. Are you using existing components and patterns where they exist? `[quality]`
8. If you are introducing new patterns, are they documented and justified? `[quality]`
9. Are design decisions consistent across screens — spacing, type, colour, interaction? `[quality]`

**Validation and risk:**
10. What assumptions are baked into this design that haven't been tested? `[risk]`
11. Is user testing planned — and are you testing with the right users at the right fidelity? `[quality]`
12. Are there any design decisions you are uncomfortable with or unsure about? `[risk]`

---

### Handover Questions — Engineering (PD → Eng)

Use when design is being handed to engineers for build.

**Completeness:**
1. Are all screen states present: default, hover, active, focus, disabled, error, empty, loading? `[quality]`
2. Are all user flows documented — happy path, error paths, edge cases? `[quality]`
3. Are all decision branches in every flow resolved? `[quality]`

**Specification quality:**
4. Are interactions and behaviours annotated — not just how it looks, but how it works? `[quality]`
5. Are design tokens and variables used — or are values hardcoded? `[quality]`
6. Is responsive behaviour specified across all relevant breakpoints? `[quality]`

**System and assets:**
7. Are all components named and mapped to the design system? `[quality]`
8. Are assets exported and named to convention? `[quality]`
9. Are any new patterns or components documented for the system? `[quality]`

**Accessibility:**
10. Does colour contrast meet WCAG AA for all text and interactive elements? `[quality]`
11. Is keyboard navigation and focus order considered? `[quality]`
12. Are there any screen reader considerations or ARIA notes for complex interactions? `[quality]`

**Handoff process:**
13. Have you walked engineers through the design — not just sent files? `[alignment]`
14. Are open questions documented and triaged by priority? `[readiness]`
15. Is there a clear point of contact for design questions during build? `[alignment]`

---

### Handover Questions — Stakeholder Review (PD → Stakeholders)

Use when presenting design for sign-off or directional review.

1. Is the problem statement visible — do stakeholders understand what problem this design solves? `[alignment]`
2. Is the design framed around user needs — or just presented as a visual? `[quality]`
3. Are key design decisions explained with rationale, not just shown? `[quality]`
4. Is it clear what is in scope and what is not — to manage expectation? `[scope]`
5. Is what is being asked of stakeholders clear — feedback on what, decision on what? `[alignment]`
6. Are known gaps or open questions disclosed? `[risk]`
7. Are next steps stated with owners and timelines? `[readiness]`

## Question Scoring Reference

When evaluating answers, apply this scale:

| Score | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ Strong | Answer is specific, grounded, and meets benchmark | Proceed |
| ⚠️ Partial | Answer exists but is vague, incomplete, or not yet validated | Note gap, proceed with flag |
| ❌ Missing | Answer absent or clearly below minimum quality bar | Block or escalate |
| 🔲 N/A | Question does not apply to this context | Skip |
